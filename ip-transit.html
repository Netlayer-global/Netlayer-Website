What is IP Transit?
Layer 3 BGP, done right.

Our IP Transit is a Layer 3 BGP connection into our Tier 2 IP backbone, enabling scalable access to the global internet. Strategically deployed across 30+ markets, we provide the best blend of IP routes for high-performance access, with full control through our global BGP communities.

How It Works
From order to advertised routes.
STEP 01
Provision the Port
We turn up a 1G, 10G or 100G port at your chosen PoP or over a Last Mile tail, with diverse options available.
STEP 02
Establish BGP
We configure an eBGP session to your ASN, apply prefix filters, and validate against IRR and RPKI ROAs.
STEP 03
Advertise & Optimise
Your prefixes go live with full BGP community control, letting you steer traffic and tune the route mix.
Technical Specifications
The detail that matters.
Port Options
1GE, 10GE, 100GE — single or LAG, with diverse/protected handoff available in major PoPs.
Protocol
eBGP (IPv4 & IPv6 dual-stack). Multi-hop and BFD supported. Full table or partial/default routes on request.
Routing Security
RPKI ROA validation, IRR-based prefix filtering, and max-prefix limits applied on every session by default.
Traffic Control
Granular BGP communities for geographic and per-peer steering, plus blackhole community for DDoS mitigation.
Commit & Burst
Fixed CIR or 95th-percentile billing. Burstable bandwidth available in select markets.
SLA
99.99% availability, 24/7 NOC, proactive monitoring, and guaranteed mean-time-to-repair targets.
